Chapter one · Material

The weight
of paper.

Every page carries the pressure of a hand. The slight valley where a pencil paused; the softened corner worried between finger and thumb. We call these imperfections, though they may be the truest record we possess.

A room
remembers.

Chapter three · Ritual

The private archive.

We keep what cannot explain itself: a receipt, a key, a sentence copied without its source. Together, these modest things become a map with no fixed north.

A note in closing

Leave a mark.
Not a monument.

The smallest gesture may travel furthest: a line beneath a sentence, a name in the back of a book, a thought left open for another hand.
